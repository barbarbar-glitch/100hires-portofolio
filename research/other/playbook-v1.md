# Cold Outreach Playbook v1

## 1) ICP definition
- **Primary segment:** B2B SaaS companies with active outbound motion.
- **Team context:** SDR/AE teams scaling pipeline with pressure on meeting quality.
- **Fit indicators:** Growth hiring, GTM process changes, new RevOps/Sales leadership, expansion targets.

## 2) Account tiering and targeting rules
- **Tier 1:** High fit + active trigger (highest personalization).
- **Tier 2:** Good fit + moderate trigger (semi-custom messaging).
- **Tier 3:** Broad fit + weak trigger (light personalization).

Targeting workflow:
1. Filter by company profile (industry, size, region, stage).
2. Add trigger signals (hiring, role changes, launch/news).
3. Map 3-5 contacts per account by persona.

## 3) Persona tracks
- **VP Sales:** cares about coverage quality, ramp consistency, pipeline risk.
- **RevOps:** cares about process consistency, attribution, handoff efficiency.
- **Marketing/Growth:** cares about channel performance and message-market fit.

## 4) Message framework
- Subject: short + signal-aware.
- Opening: why now + why this account/persona.
- Body: one business problem + one clear improvement.
- CTA: low-friction (15-minute fit check or async checklist/template).

## 5) Sequence design (14 days / 7 touches)
- Day 1: Email #1 (trigger + value + CTA)
- Day 2: LinkedIn connect/follow-up
- Day 4: Email #2 (new angle)
- Day 6: Call attempt + voicemail (if phone available)
- Day 8: LinkedIn short message
- Day 10: Email #3 (proof-driven)
- Day 14: Breakup email

## 6) Templates (starter)

### VP Sales email starter
Subject: `Quick idea on SDR ramp quality`

Hi {{FirstName}} - noticed {{Company}} is scaling SDR hiring this quarter.  
Teams at this stage often hit activity goals but meeting quality becomes inconsistent.  
We help teams tighten account selection and first-touch messaging so ramped reps book better meetings faster.  
If useful, I can share a one-page ramp QA checklist.  
Open to a 15-minute walkthrough next week?

### RevOps email starter
Subject: `Sequence consistency question`

Hi {{FirstName}} - congrats on the RevOps role at {{Company}}.  
When outbound gets standardized, the first bottleneck is usually inconsistent first-touch logic across reps.  
We help teams map trigger-to-message rules and track positive replies by segment.  
Happy to share a lightweight template your team can test in one sprint.  
Prefer a short call, or should I send it async?

## 7) KPI dashboard
- Activity: sends, calls, connection requests.
- Quality: reply rate, positive reply rate.
- Pipeline: meetings booked, show rate, meeting-to-opportunity conversion.
- Efficiency: time-to-first-positive-reply, touches per positive reply.

## 8) Weekly review cadence
- Keep: top-performing subject/opening variants.
- Improve: weak segments with low positive reply rates.
- Drop: sequences with sustained low quality despite two iterations.
- Document all changes and rationale in a weekly changelog.

## 9) Guardrails
- No fake personalization.
- No unclear value proposition.
- AI-assisted drafts require human edit for relevance and tone.
- Optimize for meaningful conversations, not just reply volume.
