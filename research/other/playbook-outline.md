# Cold Outreach Playbook Outline (Draft)

## 1) ICP and account selection
- Define tiering model: Tier 1 (high fit), Tier 2 (moderate fit), Tier 3 (volume testing).
- Use firmographic filters first (industry, size, stage, geography), then add trigger signals.
- Prioritize accounts with active change signals (hiring, funding, leadership changes, tech shifts).

## 2) Contact strategy and signal mapping
- Build 3-5 persona tracks (e.g., VP Sales, RevOps, Marketing Ops, Founder).
- Map each persona to likely pain points and measurable business outcomes.
- Assign signal-to-message rules so each first touch references a specific, relevant trigger.

## 3) Message framework
- Subject line: short, specific, and signal-driven.
- Opening: why now (trigger) + why you (role/account relevance).
- Body: one core pain + one clear value proposition.
- CTA: low-friction next step (15-minute fit check, not a hard pitch).

## 4) 7-touch multi-channel sequence (example)
- Day 1: Email #1 (trigger + value + CTA)
- Day 2: LinkedIn view/follow + soft connection request
- Day 4: Email #2 (new angle, same core problem)
- Day 6: Call attempt #1 + voicemail (if relevant)
- Day 8: LinkedIn message (short, non-pushy)
- Day 10: Email #3 (proof point / case style)
- Day 14: Breakup email (respectful close + keep-door-open)

## 5) Personalization guardrails
- Personalize with business context, not superficial profile details.
- Avoid fake personalization at scale; keep claims verifiable.
- Use AI for draft acceleration, then human-review for relevance and tone.

## 6) KPI stack and review cadence
- Activity: sends, connects, calls.
- Leading indicators: open rate, reply rate, positive reply rate.
- Pipeline indicators: meetings booked, show rate, SQO rate.
- Weekly review: kill low-performing variants, scale winners quickly.

## 7) Experiment plan (first 30 days)
- Week 1: launch baseline sequence for 2 personas.
- Week 2: test subject line and first-line variants.
- Week 3: test CTA style and sequence timing.
- Week 4: optimize by segment; document reusable templates and rules.
